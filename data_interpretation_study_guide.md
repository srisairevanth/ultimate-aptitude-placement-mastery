## 2. CONCEPT OVERVIEW

**Data Interpretation (DI)** is the art of extracting meaningful information from presented data and performing calculations to answer questions.

### What DI Tests
- **Reading comprehension of charts/tables** — can you understand what data is being shown?
- **Arithmetic speed** — percentage, ratio, average calculations under pressure
- **Approximation skills** — when exact math isn't needed
- **Logical comparison** — finding relationships between data points
- **Data sufficiency judgment** — identifying what data is relevant vs. irrelevant

### Types of Data Presented
1. **Tables** — raw numerical data in rows and columns
2. **Bar Charts** — categorical comparison (single, stacked, grouped)
3. **Line Graphs** — trend over time (single, multiple)
4. **Pie Charts** — proportion/percentage breakdown (single, multiple)
5. **Caselets** — paragraph-based data sets (textual DI)
6. **Mixed Charts** — combination of two or more chart types
7. **Radar/Spider Charts** — multi-dimensional comparison
8. **Histogram/Frequency Polygon** — distribution data

### The DI Workflow (Mavis Method)
```
Step 1 → Scan the chart: Title, Axis labels, Units, Legend
Step 2 → Understand what's being compared (absolute vs. relative)
Step 3 → Identify the question type (comparison / calculation / ratio / trend)
Step 4 → Apply the right shortcut (don't calculate what you can approximate)
Step 5 → Eliminate wrong options before calculating
Step 6 → Mark answer
```

> **Golden Rule**: In most cases, you only need to be accurate enough to pick the right option from the given choices — not to compute exact values.

## 3. CORE CONCEPTS

---

### 3A. READING A DATA SET

#### How to Read Any Chart in 30 Seconds

**For Tables:**
- Row headers = categories
- Column headers = time periods or sub-categories
- Footnotes = units, definitions, data source
- Look for: Total rows/columns, missing data, percentage rows

**For Bar/Line/Pie Charts:**
- Title tells you **what** is being measured
- Axes/legend tells you **how** it is categorized
- Units tell you **scale** (thousands vs lakhs vs millions)
- Source tells you **reliability**

**⚠️ Common Mistake**: Misreading the unit. A table showing "Revenue (in Crores)" where values look like 1,500 — is that 1,500 crores or 1.5 crores? Always check.

**⚠️ Common Mistake**: Confusing "percentage" with "percentage point." A rise from 10% to 15% is a 5 percentage point increase BUT a 50% increase relative to the original.

---

### 3B. PERCENTAGE CALCULATIONS IN DI

Most DI questions boil down to percentage operations. The key formulas:

```
Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Percentage Change = ((New - Old) / Old) × 100

Percentage Point Difference = New% - Old%

Contribution to Total (%) = (Value of Item / Total Value) × 100

Compound Growth = P × (1 + r/100)^n
```

**DI Shortcut — Percentage of a Percentage:**
```
X% of Y% of Z = (X/100) × (Y/100) × Z = (X × Y × Z) / 10,000
Example: 25% of 40% of 200 = (25 × 40 × 200) / 10,000 = 200,000 / 10,000 = 20
```

**DI Shortcut — Reverse Percentage:**
```
If X% of a number = Y, then the number = Y × 100 / X
If 12% of a number = 48, number = 48 × 100 / 12 = 400
```

---

### 3C. RATIO & PROPORTION IN DI

**Ratio from Pie Chart:**
```
Angle of sector = (Category Value / Total) × 360°
Percentage = Angle / 3.6°
```

**Shortcut — Comparing Two Ratios:**
```
To compare A/B vs C/D: Cross-multiply
A × D vs C × B
If A × D > C × B, then A/B > C/D
```

**Shortcut — Combining Ratios:**
```
If A:B = 2:3 and B:C = 4:5, find A:B:C
Make B equal: LCM of 3 and 4 = 12
A:B = 2:3 → multiply by 4 → 8:12
B:C = 4:5 → multiply by 3 → 12:15
∴ A:B:C = 8:12:15
```

---

### 3D. AVERAGE & TREND ANALYSIS

```
Simple Average = Sum of values / Number of values

Weighted Average = (Σ Weight_i × Value_i) / Σ Weight_i

Trend Growth Rate = (Final - Initial) / Initial × 100

CAGR = (Final/Initial)^(1/n) - 1 × 100
where n = number of years
```

**Trend Identification:**
- **Upward trend**: Successive increases (bullish growth)
- **Downward trend**: Successive decreases (decline)
- **Fluctuating**: Mixed — identify peak and trough
- **Plateau**: Flat — very little change over time
- **Compound growth**: Exponential — growth on growing base

---

### 3E. INTERPRETING PIE CHARTS

**Pie Chart Rules:**
1. The full circle = 100% = 360°
2. Each 1% = 3.6° of the pie
3. Sum of all slices = 360° (always verify)
4. If total is not 100%, data may be incomplete or approximated

**Comparing Two Pie Charts (Same Year vs. Different Year):**
- Calculate percentage change for each sector
- Compare growth/decline patterns
- Identify fastest/slowest growing segments

**⚠️ Common Mistake**: A larger pie slice doesn't always mean a larger absolute value — it could be that the whole pie grew. Always check the legend for absolute values or total.

---

### 3F. INTERPRETING BAR CHARTS

**Types of Bar Charts:**
- **Vertical Bar Chart**: Categories on X-axis, values on Y-axis
- **Horizontal Bar Chart**: Categories on Y-axis, values on X-axis (easier to label)
- **Stacked Bar Chart**: Shows parts of a whole within each bar
- **Grouped/Clustered Bar Chart**: Multiple bars per category for comparison
- **Bi-directional Bar Chart**: Positive and negative values on either side of axis

**Reading Stacked Bars:**
```
Total = Sum of all segments in a bar
Percentage of segment = (Segment / Total) × 100
To compare: Extract each segment separately across bars
```

---

### 3G. TABLE DATA — SPEED TECHNIQUES

**Approximation Technique for Large Numbers:**
```
Don't compute: 4,87,23,591 / 12,45,89,234
Instead estimate: 4.87 / 12.46 ≈ 0.39 ≈ 39%
```

**Shortcut for Large Division by a Near Number:**
```
18,72,350 / 24,56,800
Round both: 18.7 / 24.6
Simplify fraction: divide by 0.1
= 187/246 ≈ 0.76 ≈ 76%
```

**Cross-Multiplication for Comparison:**
```
Compare 3/7 and 5/9
3 × 9 = 27 vs 5 × 7 = 35
27 < 35 → 3/7 < 5/9
```

---

### 3H. CASESLET / TEXTUAL DI

Caselets are paragraphs with embedded data. The strategy:

```
Step 1 → Read the caselet once at normal speed (don't stop)
Step 2 → Identify: Who? What? When? Numbers? Relationships?
Step 3 → Note all given numbers in a rough column
Step 4 → Identify what the question asks
Step 5 → Only then go back to find the relevant numbers
Step 6 → Solve using basic arithmetic
```

**⚠️ Common Mistake**: Reading the caselet twice before attempting questions — wastes time. Read once, then answer questions by referencing back.

---

### 3I. MIXED CHARTS — THE TRICKY ONE

Mixed charts combine two formats (e.g., line + bar on same axes).

**Strategy:**
```
1. Identify which data corresponds to which axis/type
2. Note overlapping axes carefully
3. Don't confuse one chart's data with another's
4. Some questions compare trends from both on same axes
```

---

### 3J. INTERVIEW RELEVANCE

In interviews, DI-type questions may appear as:
- "Walk me through your analysis of [a business scenario]"
- "If this trend continues, what would you predict for next quarter?"
- "What's the most interesting insight from this data?"
- "How would you present this data to a non-technical audience?"

> **Key**: Be ready to narrate the story the data tells — not just recite numbers.

## 5. SHORTCUT TECHNIQUES

### Shortcut 1: The Elimination-First Method
> **Never calculate an exact answer if you can eliminate 3 options first.**

```
Question: What % is 487 of 1234?
Options: A) 38%  B) 39%  C) 40%  D) 41%

Eliminate: 40% of 1234 = 493.6
Our number = 487, which is slightly less than 493.6
∴ Answer is slightly less than 40% → 39% (B)
```

### Shortcut 2: The 10% Anchor Method
> **Use 10% as a mental anchor for all percentage calculations.**

```
To find X% of N:
  10% of N = N/10 (instant)
  5% of N = 10%/2
  1% of N = N/100
  20% of N = 2 × (10% of N)
  25% of N = 10%/4
  33% of N ≈ N/3
  Build up or down from 10% anchor
```

### Shortcut 3: Fraction-Decimal Equivalents (Memorize!)
```
1/2  = 50%     1/8  = 12.5%    3/8 = 37.5%
1/3  ≈ 33.3%   1/9  ≈ 11.1%    2/3 ≈ 66.7%
1/4  = 25%     1/10 = 10%      3/4 = 75%
1/5  = 20%     1/11 ≈ 9.09%    2/5 = 40%
1/6  ≈ 16.7%   1/12 ≈ 8.33%    4/5 = 80%
1/7  ≈ 14.3%   1/13 ≈ 7.69%    5/6 ≈ 83.3%
```

### Shortcut 4: Approximate Division
```
98,45,320 / 4,32,100

Step 1: 98.45 / 4.321 (round to 2 digits each)
Step 2: 98.45 / 4.321 ≈ 22.8
Step 3: Estimate answer between 22-23%
```

### Shortcut 5: Pie Chart Angle Approximation
```
Find sector angle for 27 out of 127 total
Angle = (27/127) × 360°
≈ (27/127) ≈ 0.2126
0.2126 × 360° ≈ 76.5°
Quick method: 360° / 127 ≈ 2.83° per unit
27 × 2.83° ≈ 76.4°
```

### Shortcut 6: Reading Caselets — The 3-Column Method
```
For caselet problems, quickly note:
Column 1: Entity name (A, B, C...)
Column 2: Key number associated
Column 3: Relation to other entities
This turns a paragraph into a mini-table
```

### Shortcut 7: Cross-Multiplication Comparison
```
Compare A/B and C/D quickly:
Draw an X: A───D
             ╳
           C───B
Compare A×D and B×C
Example: Compare 3/7 and 5/9
3×9=27 vs 5×7=35 → 27<35 → 3/7 < 5/9
```

### Shortcut 8: Growth Rate from Pie Charts
```
If pie chart of Year 1 shows sector = 22%
Same sector in Year 2 shows = 27%
Growth = (27-22)/22 × 100 = 22.7% increase
Not 5 percentage points — that's 22.7% actual growth
```

## 6. QUESTION PATTERNS

### PATTERN 1: Direct Value Extraction
**Pattern**: "What is the value of X in year Y?"
**Approach**: Locate the cell/bar/sector directly
**Formula**: Just read the chart — no calculation needed
**Speed**: 10 seconds

### PATTERN 2: Percentage Calculation
**Pattern**: "What percentage of X is Y?" / "What % of total is category Z?"
**Approach**: (Value of Y / Total value) × 100
**Speed**: 30-60 seconds
**Trick**: Check if the question asks for % or percentage POINTS

### PATTERN 3: Comparison Between Categories
**Pattern**: "Which category is the highest/lowest?" / "Rank the categories."
**Approach**: Compare bar heights, pie slices, or table rows
**Speed**: 20 seconds
**Trick**: In stacked bars, don't compare total heights — compare segments

### PATTERN 4: Year-over-Year Growth
**Pattern**: "What is the % growth/decline from Year A to Year B?"
**Approach**: ((New - Old) / Old) × 100
**Speed**: 60-90 seconds
**Trick**: For compound growth over multiple years, use CAGR

### PATTERN 5: Average Calculation
**Pattern**: "What is the average across categories?"
**Approach**: Sum of all values / Number of categories
**Speed**: 60 seconds
**Trick**: Watch for missing data cells — don't divide by zero

### PATTERN 6: Ratio and Proportion from Pie Chart
**Pattern**: "What is the ratio of X to Y?"
**Approach**: (Angle of X / Angle of Y) = (Value X / Value Y)
**Speed**: 45 seconds
**Trick**: For 360° pie, ratio = angle ratio directly

### PATTERN 7: Total / Subtotal from Table
**Pattern**: "What is the total of all years/regions?"
**Approach**: Row sum or column sum
**Speed**: 30-60 seconds
**Trick**: Check if a "Total" row already exists — don't recalculate

### PATTERN 8: Missing Data Inference
**Pattern**: "What is the missing value?" / "Find the value of X."
**Approach**: Use row total - known values OR use percentage relationships
**Speed**: 60-90 seconds
**Trick**: Sometimes the total row is given; subtract all other values

### PATTERN 9: Trend Analysis
**Pattern**: "Which year saw the highest/lowest growth?" / "Identify the trend."
**Approach**: Calculate growth % for each year, find max/min
**Speed**: 90 seconds
**Trick**: Sometimes the trend is visible by just looking — don't over-calculate

### PATTERN 10: Contribution to Total
**Pattern**: "What is the contribution of X to the overall total?"
**Approach**: (X / Grand Total) × 100
**Speed**: 60 seconds
**Trick**: The total may not be explicitly given — sum all parts first

### PATTERN 11: Double Pie Chart Comparison
**Pattern**: "What is the percentage point change between two pie charts?"
**Approach**: % in Chart A minus % in Chart B = percentage point change
**Speed**: 60 seconds
**⚠️ Tricky**: This is DIFFERENT from % growth of that slice

### PATTERN 12: Stacked Bar — Segment Comparison
**Pattern**: "Which year had the highest proportion of segment X?"
**Approach**: Segment X value / Total for that year
**Speed**: 90 seconds
**Trick**: Compare proportions, not absolute values

### PATTERN 13: Caselet with Two Entities
**Pattern**: "Based on the information given, what is X's share?"
**Approach**: Extract numbers for both entities, apply given relationship
**Speed**: 60-120 seconds
**Trick**: The relationship may be a ratio, percentage, or difference

### PATTERN 14: Combined Average from Multiple Sets
**Pattern**: "What is the combined average of two groups?"
**Approach**: (Avg1 × n1 + Avg2 × n2) / (n1 + n2)
**Speed**: 60 seconds
**Trick**: Don't just average the two averages — weight by counts

### PATTERN 15: Inference / Observation Questions
**Pattern**: "Which of the following can be inferred from the data?"
**Approach**: Eliminate options that contradict the data; select only what is directly supported
**Speed**: 60-90 seconds
**Trick**: Be cautious of options that are "partially true" or "mostly true" — only exact truth counts

### PATTERN 16: Prediction / Projection
**Pattern**: "If the trend continues, what will be the value in year X?"
**Approach**: Calculate average growth rate, apply to future years
**Speed**: 90 seconds
**⚠️ Tricky**: Projection assumes constant rate; mark as "approximate"

### PATTERN 17: Bar + Line Mixed Chart
**Pattern**: "What is the ratio of X (bar) to Y (line) in year Z?"
**Approach**: Read both values from their respective axes
**Speed**: 45 seconds
**Trick**: Don't mix up which axis belongs to which data

### PATTERN 18: Ranking Questions
**Pattern**: "Which of the following is the correct ranking?"
**Approach**: Extract values for all options, sort in order
**Speed**: 60-90 seconds
**Trick**: Sort mentally — don't write down all values unless necessary

### PATTERN 19: Profit/Loss Derived from Revenue & Cost Table
**Pattern**: "What is the profit % for category X?"
**Approach**: (Revenue - Cost) / Cost × 100
**Speed**: 60 seconds
**Trick**: Check units — revenue and cost may be in different rows

### PATTERN 20: Multiple Chart DI Sets (Full Set Pattern)
**Pattern**: A set of 5 questions based on a table + chart + caselet
**Approach**: Use the Mavis DI Workflow (Step 1-6 above)
**Speed**: 10-12 minutes for full set
**Trick**: Question 1 is usually the easiest (direct extraction); Question 5 is the hardest (inference/projection)

## 11. COMMON TRAPS & MISTAKES

### Trap 1: Mixing Up % and % Points
```
Wrong: Saying a change from 20% to 25% is a 5% increase
Right: It's a 25% increase (5/20) OR a 5 percentage point increase
```

### Trap 2: Forgetting to Check Units
```
A table showing values in "thousands" where values like 15 appear
= 15,000 not 15. Always check the unit label.
```

### Trap 3: Comparing Absolute Values in Stacked Bars
```
Never compare heights of stacked bars directly.
Each bar = total of segments. Compare individual segments across bars.
```

### Trap 4: Dividing by Wrong Count
```
A table with 6 rows but only 4 have data — don't divide by 6.
Only count non-empty entries.
```

### Trap 5: Over-Calculating
```
Most DI questions are designed so you can eliminate 3 options
with rough estimation. Exact calculation is often unnecessary.
```

### Trap 6: Misreading Caselets
```
Reading the caselet with the wrong assumption about what is being compared.
Re-read the question stem before going back to the caselet.
```

### Trap 7: Pie Chart Slices Not Adding to 100%
```
If total ≠ 100%, it may be due to rounding or data exclusion.
Don't assume the missing % is zero — check footnotes.
```

### Trap 8: Bar Chart Y-Axis Not Starting at Zero
```
Some bar charts truncate the Y-axis (start at 50, not 0).
This makes small differences look larger than they are.
Check the axis scale carefully.
```

## 12. PRACTICE QUESTIONS

### EASY (20 Questions)

**Q1.** A company sold 200 units in January, 350 in February, and 500 in March. What is the total sales across the three months?
- A) 1,000  B) 1,050  C) 1,100  D) 1,200

**Q2.** In a pie chart, if Category A occupies 90° of the circle, what percentage of the total does it represent?
- A) 22.5%  B) 25%  C) 30%  D) 33%

**Q3.** A table shows Product P sales of ₹4,50,000 and Product Q sales of ₹3,00,000. What is the ratio of P to Q?
- A) 2:3  B) 3:2  C) 4:3  D) 3:4

**Q4.** The population of a city was 2,00,000 in 2021 and 2,50,000 in 2022. What was the percentage increase?
- A) 20%  B) 23%  C) 25%  D) 30%

**Q5.** In a bar chart showing monthly expenses, July shows ₹12,000. If total annual expense is ₹1,44,000, what % of annual expense is July's expense?
- A) 8.3%  B) 10%  C) 12%  D) 15%

**Q6.** A table shows: Row A = 120, Row B = 80, Row C = 60, Row D = 40. What is the average value?
- A) 65  B) 70  C) 75  D) 80

**Q7.** In a pie chart, if the "Others" category is 36°, what percentage does it represent?
- A) 8%  B) 9%  C) 10%  D) 12%

**Q8.** Company X has revenue of ₹50 Lakhs and expenses of ₹30 Lakhs. What is the profit percentage?
- A) 40%  B) 50%  C) 60%  D) 66.7%

**Q9.** A line graph shows values: Jan=40, Feb=60, Mar=80. By what amount did the value increase from Jan to Mar?
- A) 20  B) 30  C) 40  D) 50

**Q10.** In a stacked bar chart, a bar has 3 segments: 25, 40, and 35. What is the total?
- A) 90  B) 95  C) 100  D) 105

**Q11.** If 30% of a population is 6,00,000, what is the total population?
- A) 15,00,000  B) 18,00,000  C) 20,00,000  D) 24,00,000

**Q12.** A table shows exports (in ₹ Crores) for 4 quarters: 120, 150, 180, 210. What is the average quarterly export?
- A) 155  B) 160  C) 165  D) 170

**Q13.** In a bar chart, Category R has value 45. The maximum value across all categories is 90. What percentage of max is R?
- A) 40%  B) 45%  C) 50%  D) 55%

**Q14.** If X is 15% of Y, and Y = 800, what is X?
- A) 100  B) 110  C) 120  D) 125

**Q15.** A pie chart shows 4 equal sectors. What is the angle of each sector?
- A) 45°  B) 60°  C) 90°  D) 120°

**Q16.** A company's revenue grew from ₹200 Cr to ₹250 Cr. What is the percentage growth?
- A) 20%  B) 23%  C) 25%  D) 30%

**Q17.** In a table, the sum of all rows is 5,000. Four rows have values 1,200, 1,500, 1,000, and 800. What is the 5th row value?
- A) 400  B) 450  C) 500  D) 550

**Q18.** A bar chart shows product sales in 5 years: 20, 25, 30, 35, 40 (in units × 1000). What is the total sales?
- A) 1,30,000  B) 1,40,000  C) 1,50,000  D) 1,60,000

**Q19.** If the total marks in an exam is 500 and a student scores 425, what percentage did they score?
